Qingyun Wu (吴青昀)

Welcome to my homepage!


I received my Ph.D. in economics from Stanford. Before coming to Stanford, I got my Bachelor's degrees in mathematics and statistics from UC Berkeley. My research interests lie primarily in game theory and market design, with technical tools from algebraic combinatorics and stochastic systems. In particular, I am interested in understanding the dynamics of reaching a market equilibrium in addition to the usual equilibrium analysis. For example, my past projects include modeling the reequilibration process of vacancy chains through Tarski's fixed point theorem; and analyzing the evolution of black markets with the help of Markov jump processes and random walks.


If you want to find out more about me, please check out my CV/Resume and research page.
